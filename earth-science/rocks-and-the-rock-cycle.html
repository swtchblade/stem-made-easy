Rocks & the Rock Cycle

How Earth's finite materials are continuously transformed through the rock cycle.

Big Idea

Earth materials are finite and are transformed over time. Igneous, metamorphic, and sedimentary rocks can change from one type to another through the rock cycle. Earth's layers have specific properties, and plate tectonics and surface processes drive these transformations.

Key Vocabulary

  • Igneous rock — rock formed from cooled and hardened magma or lava
  • Sedimentary rock — rock formed from compressed and cemented layers of sediment
  • Metamorphic rock — rock changed by extreme heat and/or pressure without melting
  • Rock cycle — the continuous process by which rocks are transformed from one type to another
  • Weathering — the breaking down of rocks by wind, water, ice, or living organisms
  • Erosion — the transport of sediment by wind, water, ice, or gravity
  • Deposition — when eroded sediments are dropped in a new location
  • Compaction & Cementation — the processes that turn loose sediments into sedimentary rock

Three Types of Rocks

🌋

Igneous Rocks

Formed when magma or lava cools and hardens.

  • Intrusive (plutonic) — cools slowly underground → large crystals (granite)
  • Extrusive (volcanic) — cools quickly on the surface → small or no crystals (basalt, obsidian)
🪨

Sedimentary Rocks

Formed from layers of sediment that are compacted and cemented over time.

  • Clastic — made of rock fragments (sandstone, shale, conglomerate)
  • Chemical — formed from dissolved minerals (limestone, rock salt)
  • Organic — formed from remains of organisms (coal, some limestone)

Most fossils are found in sedimentary rocks.

🔥

Metamorphic Rocks

Formed when existing rocks are changed by heat and/or pressure without melting.

  • Foliated — minerals align in layers/bands (slate, gneiss, schist)
  • Nonfoliated — no visible layers (marble, quartzite)

Examples: limestone → marble, shale → slate, granite → gneiss

The Rock Cycle

The rock cycle is a continuous process. Any type of rock can become any other type:

  • Igneous → weathering/erosion → sediments → Sedimentary
  • Sedimentary → heat & pressure → Metamorphic
  • Metamorphic → melting → magma → cooling → Igneous
  • Any rock can also go directly to any other step (e.g., igneous can melt again, sedimentary can be weathered again)

The rock cycle has no beginning or end — it's a continuous loop driven by Earth's internal heat and surface processes.

Earth's Layers

Each layer of Earth has rocks with specific chemical and physical properties:

  • Crust — the thin, outermost layer. Oceanic crust is thin and dense (basalt); continental crust is thick and less dense (granite)
  • Mantle — the thick middle layer. Made of hot, semi-solid rock. Contains convection currents that move tectonic plates
  • Outer core — liquid iron and nickel. Creates Earth's magnetic field
  • Inner core — solid iron and nickel. Extremely hot but kept solid by intense pressure
