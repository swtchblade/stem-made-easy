Adaptations & Survival

How organisms are built to survive in their specific ecosystem.

Big Idea

Adaptations are physical or behavioral traits that help an organism survive and reproduce in its environment. Biotic and abiotic factors define the conditions of land, marine, and freshwater ecosystems, and organisms are adapted to live in specific ones.

Key Vocabulary

  • Adaptation — a trait that helps an organism survive and reproduce in its environment
  • Physical (structural) adaptation — a body part or physical feature that helps survival (e.g., thick fur, webbed feet)
  • Behavioral adaptation — an action or behavior that helps survival (e.g., migration, hibernation)
  • Camouflage — body coloring or pattern that helps an organism blend in with its surroundings
  • Mimicry — when one species looks like another species to gain protection
  • Habitat — the specific place where an organism lives
  • Biome — a large region of Earth with similar climate, plants, and animals

Types of Ecosystems

Ecosystems are defined by their biotic (living) and abiotic (nonliving) factors. The three major categories are:

🌲

Land (Terrestrial)

  • Forest — lots of trees, moderate rainfall
  • Desert — very little rain, extreme temperatures
  • Grassland — mostly grasses, few trees, moderate rain
  • Tundra — very cold, frozen soil (permafrost), few plants
  • Rainforest — very warm, heavy rainfall, high biodiversity
🌊

Marine (Saltwater)

  • Ocean — largest ecosystem, covers 70% of Earth
  • Coral reefs — warm, shallow water, high biodiversity
  • Estuaries — where freshwater meets saltwater
  • Deep sea — dark, cold, high pressure
🏞️

Freshwater

  • Lakes and ponds — still water
  • Rivers and streams — flowing water
  • Wetlands — marshes, swamps; saturated soil

Freshwater is only about 3% of Earth's water but supports a huge variety of life.

Physical (Structural) Adaptations

These are body features that help an organism survive:

  • Thick fur or blubber — keeps animals warm in cold environments (polar bear, walrus)
  • Large ears — release body heat in hot environments (fennec fox)
  • Webbed feet — help with swimming (duck, frog)
  • Sharp claws or teeth — help predators catch prey (eagle, lion)
  • Thorns or spines — protect plants from being eaten (cactus, porcupine)
  • Camouflage — blending into surroundings (chameleon, Arctic hare)
  • Mimicry — looking like a dangerous species for protection (viceroy butterfly mimics the monarch)

Behavioral Adaptations

These are actions or behaviors that help organisms survive:

🦅

Migration

Moving to a different area when seasons change. Animals migrate to find better food, water, or breeding grounds.

Examples: birds flying south in winter, whales migrating to warmer waters, wildebeest in Africa

💤

Hibernation

Entering a deep sleep-like state during cold months to conserve energy when food is scarce.

Examples: bears, ground squirrels, bats

🌙

Nocturnal Behavior

Being active at night to avoid extreme heat or daytime predators.

Examples: owls, bats, raccoons

🐾

Other Behaviors

  • Playing dead — opossums avoid predators by pretending to be dead
  • Territorial marking — animals mark areas to avoid competition
  • Courtship displays — peacock feathers attract mates
