Genetics & Heredity

How traits are passed from parents to offspring through DNA, genes, and reproduction.

Big Idea

Organisms reproduce and transmit genetic information to new generations. DNA contains the instructions for making proteins that determine an organism's traits. Meiosis ensures genetic diversity, and Punnett squares predict the probability of inherited traits.

Key Vocabulary

  • DNA (deoxyribonucleic acid) — the molecule inside cells that carries genetic instructions for growth, development, and reproduction
  • Gene — a section of DNA that codes for a specific protein or trait
  • Chromosome — a tightly coiled structure of DNA; humans have 46 chromosomes (23 pairs)
  • Trait — a characteristic of an organism (e.g., eye color, height, flower color)
  • Allele — different forms of the same gene (e.g., "B" for brown eyes, "b" for blue eyes)
  • Dominant allele — the allele that is expressed when at least one copy is present (uppercase letter, e.g., B)
  • Recessive allele — the allele that is only expressed when two copies are present (lowercase letter, e.g., b)
  • Genotype — the genetic makeup of an organism (e.g., BB, Bb, or bb)
  • Phenotype — the physical appearance or observable trait (e.g., brown eyes)
  • Homozygous — having two identical alleles (BB or bb)
  • Heterozygous — having two different alleles (Bb)

DNA Makes Proteins That Determine Traits

The DNA inside every cell contains instructions, like a recipe book. Here is how DNA leads to traits:

  • DNA is made of a specific sequence of bases (A, T, C, G)
  • A gene is a section of DNA with a specific base sequence
  • The gene provides instructions to build a specific protein
  • Proteins control chemical reactions and build cell structures
  • Proteins determine traits — like hair color, blood type, or plant height

DNA → Gene → Protein → Trait

Meiosis — Making Sex Cells

Meiosis is a type of cell division that produces sex cells (eggs and sperm), also called gametes.

Key Facts About Meiosis

  • Meiosis produces cells with half the normal number of chromosomes (23 in humans instead of 46)
  • When an egg and sperm join during fertilization, the full number of chromosomes is restored (46)
  • Each sex cell has a unique combination of genes → this creates genetic diversity
  • Meiosis involves two divisions and produces 4 cells

Meiosis vs. Mitosis

  • Mitosis — produces 2 identical cells (for growth and repair). All body cells.
  • Meiosis — produces 4 unique cells with half the chromosomes (for reproduction). Sex cells only.

Punnett Squares

A Punnett square is a math tool used to predict the probability of offspring having certain traits.

How to Use a Punnett Square

  • Write the alleles of one parent across the top
  • Write the alleles of the other parent along the side
  • Fill in each box by combining the alleles from the row and column
  • Count the results to find the ratios of genotypes and phenotypes

Example: Flower Color

Parent 1: Bb (heterozygous purple)
Parent 2: Bb (heterozygous purple)

Bb
BBBBb
bBbbb

Result: 75% purple (BB or Bb), 25% white (bb)
Genotype ratio: 1 BB : 2 Bb : 1 bb
