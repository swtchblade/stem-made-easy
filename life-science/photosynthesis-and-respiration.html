Photosynthesis & Cellular Respiration

The chemical processes of energy transfer that power all living things.

Big Idea

Photosynthesis is the foundation of virtually all food webs. Together, photosynthesis and cellular respiration transfer energy and support all life processes on Earth.

Key Vocabulary

  • Photosynthesis — the process plants use to convert sunlight, water, and carbon dioxide into glucose (food) and oxygen
  • Cellular respiration — the process cells use to break down glucose and release energy (ATP) for life activities
  • Chloroplast — the organelle in plant cells where photosynthesis takes place
  • Chlorophyll — the green pigment in chloroplasts that absorbs sunlight
  • Mitochondria — the organelle where cellular respiration takes place; the "powerhouse" of the cell
  • Glucose — a simple sugar (C₆H₁₂O₆) that stores chemical energy; made during photosynthesis
  • ATP (adenosine triphosphate) — the molecule cells use as energy currency; produced during cellular respiration
  • Autotroph (Producer) — an organism that makes its own food through photosynthesis
  • Heterotroph (Consumer) — an organism that gets energy by eating other organisms

How Photosynthesis Works

Photosynthesis happens in the chloroplasts of plant cells. Plants capture light energy from the sun and use it to combine carbon dioxide (CO₂) from the air and water (H₂O) from the soil.

The Equation

6CO₂ + 6H₂O + Light Energy → C₆H₁₂O₆ + 6O₂

Carbon dioxide + Water + Sunlight → Glucose + Oxygen

What Goes In:

  • Carbon dioxide (from the air)
  • Water (from the soil through roots)
  • Sunlight (absorbed by chlorophyll)

What Comes Out:

  • Glucose (food / stored chemical energy)
  • Oxygen (released into the air)

Why Photosynthesis Matters

🌿

Foundation of Food Webs

Almost all food chains start with a plant or other photosynthetic organism. Producers capture energy from the sun and pass it to consumers when they are eaten.

🌬️

Produces Oxygen

Photosynthesis releases oxygen into the atmosphere. This is the oxygen that animals (including humans) breathe to stay alive.

♻️

Part of the Carbon Cycle

Plants remove carbon dioxide from the air during photosynthesis, helping to regulate Earth's atmosphere and climate.

How Cellular Respiration Works

Cellular respiration happens in the mitochondria of all living cells (both plant and animal). Cells break down glucose using oxygen to release energy (ATP).

The Equation

C₆H₁₂O₆ + 6O₂ → 6CO₂ + 6H₂O + Energy (ATP)

Glucose + Oxygen → Carbon dioxide + Water + Energy

Notice that cellular respiration is essentially the reverse of photosynthesis. The products of one process are the reactants of the other.

How They Work Together

Photosynthesis and cellular respiration are opposite but connected processes. They form a cycle:

  • Photosynthesis builds glucose and releases oxygen
  • Cellular respiration breaks down glucose and uses oxygen
  • The oxygen released by plants is used by animals for respiration
  • The CO₂ released by animals is used by plants for photosynthesis

Both plants and animals carry out cellular respiration. Only plants (and some other organisms like algae) carry out photosynthesis.
