Electricity and Magnetism

Charges, circuits, magnets, and how electricity and magnetism are connected.

Big Idea

Electricity and magnetism are closely related forces. Moving electric charges create magnetic fields, and moving magnets can generate electric current. Together they form electromagnetism.

Static Electricity

  • Electric charge — a property of matter; can be positive (+) or negative (-)
  • Static electricity — the buildup of electric charge on an object
  • Like charges repel; opposite charges attract
  • Charging by friction — rubbing two objects together transfers electrons from one to the other
  • Electric discharge — the sudden flow of charge between two objects (e.g., lightning, a static shock)

Electric Current and Circuits

  • Electric current — the flow of electric charges through a conductor
  • Voltage — the force that pushes electric charges through a circuit (measured in Volts)
  • Resistance — opposition to the flow of current (measured in Ohms)
  • Conductor — a material that allows electric current to flow easily (e.g., metals)
  • Insulator — a material that does not allow current to flow easily (e.g., rubber, plastic)

Circuit Components

  • Battery / Power source — provides voltage
  • Wire — conductor that carries current
  • Load — device that uses electrical energy (e.g., a light bulb)
  • Switch — opens or closes the circuit

Series vs. Parallel Circuits

➡️

Series Circuit

  • Components connected in a single path
  • If one part breaks, the whole circuit stops
  • Current is the same everywhere
  • Voltage is divided among components
🔀

Parallel Circuit

  • Components connected in multiple paths
  • If one part breaks, the others still work
  • Voltage is the same across each path
  • Current is divided among paths

Magnetism

  • Magnet — an object that attracts iron and certain other metals
  • Magnetic field — the invisible area around a magnet where its force can act
  • Magnetic poles — every magnet has a North and South pole; like poles repel, opposite poles attract
  • Electromagnet — a temporary magnet created by running electric current through a coil of wire

Electromagnetism in Action

⚙️

Electric Motors

Convert electrical energy into mechanical energy (movement). A current-carrying wire in a magnetic field experiences a force that makes it spin.

🔌

Generators

Convert mechanical energy into electrical energy. Moving a magnet through a coil of wire (or vice versa) induces an electric current.

🔄

Transformers

Increase or decrease voltage in power lines. Used to transmit electricity over long distances efficiently.
