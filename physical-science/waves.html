Waves

How energy travels through matter — transverse and longitudinal wave motion.

Big Idea

Waves are a way energy moves from one place to another without moving matter long distances. Waves are used in sound, light, communication, and many everyday technologies.

Energy Transfer by Longitudinal and Transverse Waves

Key Vocabulary

  • Wave — a repeating movement that transfers energy
  • Transverse wave — wave where motion is perpendicular to energy direction
  • Longitudinal wave — wave where motion is parallel to energy direction
  • Medium — the matter through which a wave travels
  • Mechanical wave — a wave that requires a medium (e.g., sound, water waves)

Wave Parts and Properties

Transverse Wave Parts

  • Crest — the highest point of a transverse wave
  • Trough — the lowest point of a transverse wave

Longitudinal Wave Parts

  • Compression — area where particles are pushed close together
  • Rarefaction — area where particles are spread apart

Key Properties

  • Wavelength — the distance from one crest to the next (or one compression to the next)
  • Frequency — the number of waves that pass a point per second (measured in Hertz, Hz)
  • Amplitude — the height of a wave from the rest position; related to energy

Wavelength and Frequency Relationship

As wavelength increases, frequency decreases (and vice versa). They have an inverse relationship.

Higher frequency = more energy. Higher amplitude = more energy.
