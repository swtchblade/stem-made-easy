Electromagnetic Radiation

The full spectrum of energy — from radio waves to gamma rays.

Big Idea

Electromagnetic (EM) waves are transverse waves that can travel through empty space (no medium needed). They travel at the speed of light and carry energy across the universe.

Key Vocabulary

  • Electromagnetic wave — a transverse wave made of vibrating electric and magnetic fields
  • Electromagnetic spectrum — the complete range of EM waves organized by wavelength and frequency
  • Wavelength — the distance between two consecutive crests of a wave
  • Frequency — the number of wave cycles per second (measured in Hertz)
  • Speed of light — approximately 300,000 km/s; all EM waves travel at this speed in a vacuum

The Electromagnetic Spectrum

From longest wavelength / lowest frequency to shortest wavelength / highest frequency:

  • Radio waves — used for radio, TV, and cell phone signals
  • Microwaves — used for cooking and satellite communication
  • Infrared (IR) — felt as heat; used in remote controls and thermal cameras
  • Visible light — the only EM waves we can see (red → violet)
  • Ultraviolet (UV) — causes sunburns; used to sterilize equipment
  • X-rays — used to see inside the body (bones and teeth)
  • Gamma rays — highest energy; used in cancer treatment and emitted by radioactive materials

Frequency, Wavelength, and Energy

As you move from radio waves → gamma rays:

  • Wavelength decreases
  • Frequency increases
  • Energy increases

Remember: wavelength and frequency have an inverse relationship, but frequency and energy have a direct relationship.
