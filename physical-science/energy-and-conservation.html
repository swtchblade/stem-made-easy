Energy and Conservation

How energy is stored, transferred, and transformed — but never created or destroyed.

Big Idea

Energy cannot be created or destroyed, but it can be stored, transferred, and transformed. This rule is called the law of conservation of energy.

Energy Can Be Stored in Different Ways

Key Vocabulary

  • Energy — the ability to do work or cause change
  • Stored energy — energy held in an object or system
  • Potential energy — stored energy based on position or shape
  • Gravitational potential energy — stored energy due to height above the ground
  • Elastic potential energy — stored energy in a stretched or compressed object
  • Chemical energy — stored energy in the bonds of molecules (found in food, fuel, and batteries)

Energy in Motion

  • Kinetic energy — the energy of motion; the faster an object moves, the more kinetic energy it has
  • Mechanical energy — energy of movement (e.g., a moving car, a thrown ball)
  • Thermal energy (heat) — energy from the movement of particles inside matter; more movement = higher temperature
  • Light energy — energy carried by electromagnetic waves
  • Sound energy — energy carried by vibrations through matter

Energy Transformations

Energy can change from one form to another. Here are some everyday examples:

🍎 Eating Food

Chemical energy in food → Mechanical energy for movement + Thermal energy (body heat)

💡 Turning on a Lamp

Electrical energy → Light energy + Thermal energy (heat from the bulb)

🎸 Playing Guitar

Mechanical energy (plucking strings) → Sound energy

🌿 Photosynthesis

Light energy from the sun → Chemical energy stored in glucose
