Organization in Living Things

From cells to organisms — how life is structured at every level.

Big Idea

Living things are organized from simple to complex: Cells → Tissues → Organs → Organ Systems → Organisms. Each level works together to keep the organism alive.

Levels of Organization

  • Cell — the basic unit of life (e.g., a red blood cell)
  • Tissue — a group of similar cells working together (e.g., muscle tissue)
  • Organ — a structure made of different tissues working together (e.g., the heart)
  • Organ System — a group of organs that perform a major function (e.g., the circulatory system)
  • Organism — a complete living thing made of all organ systems working together

Unicellular vs. Multicellular

🦠

Unicellular Organisms

Made of one cell that performs all life functions. Examples include bacteria and amoebas. The single cell must take in food, remove waste, reproduce, and respond to the environment.

🧬

Multicellular Organisms

Made of many cells that are specialized for different jobs. Examples include plants, animals, and humans. Cells are organized into tissues, organs, and organ systems.

Plant Structures

Plants also have organized structures that help them survive:

  • Roots — absorb water and nutrients from the soil; anchor the plant
  • Stems — support the plant and transport water and nutrients between roots and leaves
  • Leaves — the main site of photosynthesis; capture sunlight to make food
  • Flowers — reproductive structures that produce seeds
